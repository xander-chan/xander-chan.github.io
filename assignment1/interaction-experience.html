Interaction Experience with Minjaeseo.com

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?:

The first thing I noticed about this website was its clean and minimalistic nature. The simple colour palette of black, white and grey made the content visually appealing and easy to understand. Reinforcing this, the professional but simple looking font contributed to the overall unified design. There was no overload of information, and the buttons and text were all well spaced with padding. Even the background image looked like it was specifically designed for this website as it had a distinct style, portraying that she was in complete control of the aesthetic.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions:

  • Scrolled down the page to explore content and saw if there was any special animations.
  • Observed the cursor to check for uniqueness as seen with the other featured websites.
  • Clicked the ‘Explore Projects’ button to see where it led - went down the page.
  • Clicked the ‘See My Resume’ button, which opened a separate Google Drive page containing the resume.
  • Examined how different sections were linked and structured within the site.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?:

Since the website is relatively simple, I spent the most time clicking through the navigation buttons and viewing the projects and resume. Nothing really popped out at the user, but that isn’t necessarily a bad thing, the website only consisted of the core important information to effectively get her professional experience across.

What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?:

Clicking different buttons to navigate through the portfolio and access additional information.

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?:

The primary goal of the website is to serve as a professional portfolio for Min Jea Seo. It showcases projects and career achievements in a structured, minimalistic way, making it easy for potential employers or clients to see whether she has the potential to design/create a website of their choosing.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?:

The design is clear and focused, with a strong emphasis on functionality. The main navigation elements - exploring her previous projects and accessing her resume full of experience are positioned to the centre of the screen so that users can quickly find the most relevant content. There are no ‘cool things’ but it also means that the user won’t get confused or distracted.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?:

The website is designed for short-term engagement rather than frequent revisits. Users are likely to visit once or twice to review Min Jea Seo’s work before deciding on potential collaboration or employment.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?:

There aren’t any features the encourage the user to come back and keep interacting with the website as the user can’t really comment on any of the content shown. Instead, it’s more a static portfolio that strictly concentrates on it being proof of her works rather than a continuous evolving, updated page.

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?:

The site takes inspiration from traditional resumes and professional portfolios. The clean layout resembles high-end design websites and has a conventional layout, mirroring corporate platforms like LinkedIn.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

It suggests that users such as myself should treat it as a portfolio rather than a fun entertaining experience. Since it mimics a typical informative website it encourages users to take an employer perspective and glance at her redesigns and plugins to assess whether it fits the wants and needs a usual business website requires.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The minimalistic design and structured content create a sense of professionalism and credibility. It does not evoke excitement or playfulness but rather creates confidence in the viewer towards Min Jea Seo’s expertise.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part about this website is that it’s clear she’s very skilled but hasn’t added any twists to make it more appealing. There was just an overall lack of interactive elements or unique design choices that showcase her creativity. Even though that is what an employer usually wants she might not be able to distinguish herself from the other web developers that can do this as well. Adding slight animations or even the easiest interactive elements could make this website more memorable, otherwise there is nothing really special.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The ease of navigation and clarity of content were the most satisfying aspects. The website loads quickly, buttons function smoothly, and information is well-organised, making it a seamless experience for me when I was trying to look through everything the website contained.
